## 0 · Why this artifact exists

GuestIQ defined value carefully for the **GM** (the buyer — credibility to act) and the **agent** (the contributor — motivation to contribute candidly). The **researcher** — the person who actually runs the pilot — was treated as *plumbing*: "the one who runs the pipeline." That is the same omission we made for the agent early on, one layer up, and it matters more here than it sounds: **for a pilot, the researcher's job is the entire point.** The GM report is an *artifact the researcher studies*; the agent experience is a *mechanism the researcher is testing*. Neither is the researcher's deliverable. This model defines what the researcher actually needs — so their outputs are derived from defined value, not invented because a dashboard "feels useful."

## 1 · The design north star

> **The researcher's reward is a confident verdict** — *did GuestIQ produce findings the GM judged non-obvious and actionable, on participation healthy and data clean enough to trust* — **and a grounded decision on what to change next.**

The researcher is the **pilot's judge, not its operator.** (Automating the GM report — generated by the system, self-served by the GM — is precisely what frees the researcher from operating the pipeline to *judging* the result.) The judge's instrument is the **Researcher Console** (§5.0) — one in-app surface giving the full picture: is the pilot *working* (software, agents, buyer), is the data *trustworthy*, did it *deliver value*, and what to *change*.

## 2 · Who we design for

The **Lead Researcher** — deep hospitality domain expertise, non-technical, owns the integrity of the output and the decision about GuestIQ's future. Runs a short pilot at one property; needs to come away knowing whether the instrument *works* and what to iterate. Not a data engineer; needs answers, not raw plumbing.

## 3 · What makes the researcher different

The three stakeholders need three different things:

| Stakeholder | Needs | To… |
|---|---|---|
| **Agent** | **motivation** | contribute candidly, return within the window |
| **GM** | **credibility** | trust the findings and act |
| **Researcher** | **confidence** | judge whether it worked and decide what's next |

The agent and GM are **subjects** of the pilot; the researcher is the **experimenter.** The researcher is the only one who sees *behind the curtain* (participation, integrity) — and must **never leak that** to the other two.

## 4 · The researcher's jobs-to-be-done

**Primary JTBD**
> *When I run a GuestIQ pilot, I want to know whether the instrument produced findings the GM judges **non-obvious and actionable** — on participation **healthy** and data **clean** enough to trust — so that I can decide whether GuestIQ works and what to change.*

**Sub-jobs**
- **J1 — Monitor the pilot (is it working?).** *When the pilot is running, I want to see whether the whole thing is working across its three moving parts — the **software** held up, the **agents** took to it, and the **buyer** engaged — so that I can trust the run and catch problems early.* Three lenses: **app health · agent activity & engagement · GM activity & engagement.**
- **J2 — Trust the output (integrity).** *When I read the output, I want to know the data is clean — distinct counts honest, small-N respected, free-text not over-read — so that I can stand behind the findings.*
- **J3 — Judge the product (validation).** *When the report exists, I want to learn whether the GM found it non-obvious and worth acting on — so that I know if the product delivered its core value (the North Star).*
- **J4 — Decide what's next (learning).** *When the pilot ends, I want a clear read on what worked and what didn't — so that I can iterate the instrument, the gold map, and the thresholds.*

## 5 · The value model — what each job needs

### 5.0 · The Researcher Console (the single in-app surface)
All researcher value is delivered through **one in-app surface — the Researcher Console** — locked behind a **researcher PIN** (separate from the GM's). Everything is in-app; no external dashboards. Six lenses map to the jobs: **App Health · Agent Activity · GM Activity** (J1) · **Integrity** (J2) · **Validation** (J3) · **Iteration** (J4). *(Reference: Researcher-Console mockup v2.)*

### 5.1 · J1 → The three monitoring lenses
- **App health — did the software hold up?** Availability, errors/crashes, failed saves, response times, and device/browser issues (incl. iOS Safari/WebKit, which desktop emulation misses). *A brilliant report no one can submit is a failed pilot.*
- **Agent activity & engagement — did they take to it?** **Per badge**: reads, time per persona, time in app, sessions, depth opt-in, replay; plus coverage per guest type, **completion rate** (the health metric), and drop-off. Per-badge timings are a **research signal, not performance** (§6, §8).
- **GM activity & engagement — did the buyer engage?** Report opens, recency, dwell, **re-opens** (the strongest unsolicited signal the findings mattered), and export — from the open-log (FR-GMA-05).

### 5.2 · J2 → Integrity checks
The **badge-fragmentation anomaly check**, **small-N / convergence-floor compliance**, **gold-tag / CF-sink suppression integrity**, and **guardrail compliance**. **Free-text is never coded — it is *count-or-quote*** (structured options count themselves; verbatim items and optional examples appear as quotes — Questionnaire v4.2 / Output-Spec v0.2). The engine is **deterministic and computed on-open — no AI**. This is a **check on that output** for the researcher's confidence — **never a gate on the GM's access** (§6).

### 5.3 · J3 → Validation capture (the North Star)
**Did the GM find the findings non-obvious and worth acting on, and which landed?** Captured by a **light structured "GM reaction" form in the Console** — a few fixed questions per finding (*non-obvious? · would act?*), filled by the researcher after walking the GM through the report. Structured enough that pilots are comparable and the North Star is measurable; the **GM never sees it**. The report still auto-generates and self-serves — this capture is the researcher's separate validation instrument, not a delivery gate.

### 5.4 · J4 → Iteration inputs
What the above say to change: instrument items, gold-map priors, convergence thresholds, **app fixes** (e.g., the iOS Safari issue), and design surfaces — each traceable to a lens above.

## 6 · Boundaries (the disciplines)

- **The GM report is automatic and self-served.** The researcher is **not** in its generation or delivery path (the engine produces it; the GM opens it via Ctrl+Alt+A + PIN).
- **Everything researcher-facing lives in the in-app Console, behind a researcher PIN** — invisible to the GM and to agents.
- **Per-badge activity is a research signal, never performance** — never a ranking, never shown to agents or the GM, never used to reward velocity.
- **Signal, not proof.** Timings and opens are signals to interpret (time = engaged *or* stuck; fast = fluent *or* rushing; opens = attention, not agreement) — never scores.
- **Anonymity holds even for the researcher.** Badges are pseudonymous (no PII, no key); per-badge means "Fox," never a real person — the researcher cannot deanonymize.
- **The validation capture is a research instrument, not a delivery gate.**

## 7 · Where the previously-homeless decisions land

| Decision (previously unhomed) | Home |
|---|---|
| **Completion rate = health metric** (not the North Star) | J1 · §5.1 |
| **North Star = non-obvious findings delivered** (how it's *measured*) | J3 · §5.3 |
| **Badge-fragmentation anomaly check** | J2 · §5.2 |
| **GM reaction = validation signal** | J3 · §5.3 |

These were homeless because the researcher had no value model to live in. They do now.

## 8 · Guardrails (non-negotiable)
Researcher outputs never reach the GM or agents · per-badge activity is **never a ranking** and never rewards velocity · timings and opens are **signals to interpret, not scores** · the Console never identifies a real individual (pseudonymous badges only) · the integrity review never gates the GM's auto-served report (it informs iteration, not delivery) · the validation capture records reaction, never coaches the GM toward a verdict · no researcher need ever weakens the convergence floor or the gold map to manufacture findings (anti-metric, shared with the North Star doc).

## 9 · The falsifiable value test
> *The researcher runs a pilot, reads a clean participation picture and an integrity-checked dataset, and learns directly whether the GM found the findings non-obvious and actionable — **so that** they can issue a **confident verdict** on whether GuestIQ works and a **grounded decision** on what to change.*
Any researcher-facing output that can't point back to this sentence is decoration.

## 10 · How it fits the system
The **Console** reads from tools we already run: **Sentry** (app health), **PostHog** (agent + GM activity), the **report open-log** (FR-GMA-05), the **report engine** (findings, integrity), and the **dataset export**. This model defines the *needs*; the **Observability spec defines the events** that feed the Console's J1 lenses. The researcher studies the GM's report and tests the agent's experience, but holds a surface neither of them sees.
