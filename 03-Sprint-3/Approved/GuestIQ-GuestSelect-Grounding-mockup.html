Reading · the Business guest
Picture one business traveller you know well. What do they quietly notice — but would never mention at the desk?
The first question does the grounding — then every question after just says "they."
The design calls here:

1 · Guest-select is where "do I know this guest?" is decided — the agent only picks a type they've read many times, which is why the read screen needs no per-question skip.
2 · Nine personas from the v4.2 routing — Business · Early flight/late arrival · Holiday · Cruise · Event · Medical · Airline crew · Executive/VIP · Someone else — each with a one-line "is this you?" descriptor. (Descriptors approved for The Cascade's mix.)
3 · No separate grounding screen (decided — save the step). Instead, the first question carries the grounding ("Picture one business traveller you know well…"); every question after it simply refers to "they." The projection prime is kept; the extra tap is gone.
4 · No PII trap. The agent holds the guest in their own head — GuestIQ never asks for a name, room, or any real detail. Grounding stays private to the agent.

Spec note for Claude Code: the grounding lead-in shows only on the first question of a read; Q2+ drop it.
