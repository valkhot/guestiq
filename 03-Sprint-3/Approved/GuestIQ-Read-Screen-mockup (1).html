The design calls in here, for your reaction:

1 · One question at a time. Calm and ESL-friendly over a dense grid — the agent stays in the guest's head, not scanning a form.
2 · The persona chip stays pinned ("Reading · the Business guest") so they never drift out of projection.
3 · No global skip. The "do I know this guest?" decision happens once, at guest-select — so there's no per-question skip to clutter the screen. The few questions that need a gentle "not sure" carry it as a normal option in their own list.
4 · No progress-as-game. Just the quiet dot rail — no filling bar, no count-up, nothing that turns reading a guest into a score.
5 · Gold tags invisible. Nothing on screen hints which answer is "valuable" — that bias would wreck the data. Every structured question is the same shape: ~10 options (9–10 gold + 1 quiet "usual" CF-sink) + one other. The CF-sink here ("the room not clean enough · the usual") is collected but suppressed from findings, and looks like any other row.
6 · One free-text per question — other, prominent. Below the list, an open writing area ("Or write it in their own words") makes it obvious the agent can type — captured verbatim (count-or-quote). The structured options still come first, but typing is clearly invited, not hidden.

✅ Decided: one question at a time (not grouped). Still open: is the free-text now prominent enough — and does a full battery of ~10-option screens feel the right length, or should some questions be trimmed?
