## **2. Testing Guide — How to Test as You Go**

You asked for guidance on testing. This section explains what testing means in this project, why it matters, and how to do it — before the individual story cards show you the specific steps for each story.


## **What testing means in ****GuestIQ**

Testing means checking that what was built actually does what it was supposed to do. It sounds obvious, but it is easy to skip — and when you skip it, bugs reach the pilot. A bug found during testing takes 20 minutes to fix. A bug found during the pilot with 10 front desk staff takes days of damage control.

There are two types of testing you will do:

• Functional testing — does the feature work? Click through the flow and check the result matches the acceptance criteria.

• Data testing — did the right data land in Supabase? Open the Table Editor and verify the rows.


## **The testing mindset**

When you test, you are not trying to confirm it works. You are trying to find where it breaks. This is an important distinction. Test the obvious path, then test the edge cases:

• What happens if I click None of these fit on every question?

• What happens if I answer Q1 as Work-Transit and then go through Module 5 — do I get section 5A (work)?

• What happens if I close the browser halfway through and reopen — does the disambiguation screen appear?

• What happens in Amateur tier vs Expert tier — are the right questions showing?


## **How to read the testing guide in each story card**

Each story card below has a purple testing guide section. It tells you exactly what to do to verify that story — step by step, in plain English. You do not need technical knowledge to follow these steps. You need:

• The app running locally at http://localhost:5174/guestiq/

• The Supabase Table Editor open in another browser tab

• The PostHog Activity view open in a third tab (optional but useful)

After each AI delivery, work through the testing guide for that story before telling me it is done. If something does not match, take a screenshot and share it — that is a bug report.


## **What to do when you find a bug**

When the app does not behave as the testing guide says it should:

1.  Take a screenshot of what you see.

2.  Note exactly what you did — which tier, which question, which option you clicked.

3.  Note what you expected vs what actually happened.

4.  Share this with me and I will diagnose and fix it.

5.  After the fix is pushed, re-run the same test to confirm it is resolved.


⚠ WARN:  Do not move to the next story until the current story's testing guide passes. A bug in S3-01 (Module 5 routing) will cause wrong data in every subsequent session — fixing it after S3-13 (data integrity pass) is much harder than fixing it immediately.
