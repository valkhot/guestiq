How this wall behaves
Gaps first. "Not yet" frames sit at the top — where you can still help is the first thing you see; the guests already known well settle to the bottom.
Status only, never numbers. Frames read Do you know them? · Started · Known well — never "12 reads," never a ranking. The empty frames ask a real question rather than command, so an agent who doesn't know that guest just moves on — no thin, guessy data.
Your reads are marked, not separated. The ⬡ badge pins the guests you've put on record right on the desk-wide wall — so no separate "mine" tab is needed. Your own portfolio is celebrated at the end-of-read gallery instead. (A deliberate simplification of Value Model §4.2's two-view toggle — the pins + gallery cover it.)
Completed-range never nags. When you've read every guest you truly know, it reframes to "this is plenty," and the rest become the desk's to fill — never "you're unfinished."
No promises, no depth here. It doesn't claim your reads will become findings (that recognition comes collectively, at the GM debrief), and going deeper is offered mid-read only.
