The design calls here
1 · Claimed once, then just tapped. First run: pick from the 12. Every shift after, the agent doesn't re-claim — they land on "Welcome back, Fox" and tap through.
2 · Named at the moment of claim. "You're the Fox" — the animal-only name (colour stays visual). It's reinforced later at grounding ("Alright, Fox").
3 · Identity, never a score. The claim screen says it plainly — not a name, not a score, just you — and there's no PII, no login, no key. It can't identify the person; it only marks that a read is theirs.
4 · A gentle way out. On a shared PC the last badge might not be yours — "Not the Fox? Switch badge" lets the next person claim/select theirs without confusion.
Plain-language (NFR-TONE-02): "Pick up where you left off" → "Continue where you stopped". "Switch badge" reads clearly and stays.
