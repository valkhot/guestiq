## The two new segments

**CW — Airline crew.** Pilots and flight attendants on a layover. **GM's prior (strong): "Crew isn't our segment."** Crew layovers are usually **airport hotels under airline contracts**, not a downtown branded house. Any crew we see is incidental (repositioning, a crew member choosing downtown on personal time, a charter).
→ *This prior may be **correct** (like the fitness center). The first thing the pilot tests is whether CW exists here in any volume at all. **If it doesn't clear the floor, that is a valid CONFIRM** ("crew isn't a real segment for you"), not a failure. Gold exists **only if** the desk actually sees crew volume — then their needs (below) are a genuine blind spot.*

**VX — Executive / VIP / celebrity.** Senior executives (Seattle tech/board travel), the occasional celebrity or performer (event-driven), high-tier loyalty guests. **GM's prior (strong): "We handle VIPs with our program — an upgrade, a welcome amenity, a manager greeting, a PMS VIP flag."** The GM believes VIP care = **visible recognition**.
→ *Real segment (execs are common; the celebrity slice is thin and event-driven). The gold is that the GM's recognition-based playbook may be **backwards** for much of this segment — see §15.*

## New-persona rows on existing topics

**Topic 2 · Why they really choose us**
- **CW** → if present at all: **airport proximity + airline contract + reliability**, not brand or downtown location. For a downtown house this mostly explains *why crew DON'T choose us.* *BLIND-SPOT · partial · conditional on volume*
- **VX** → discretion, security, and a trusted point of contact — **not** the amenity or the loyalty perk. *MIS-WEIGHT · Y*

**Topic 3 · Why we lose them (turnaway)**
- **CW** → lost by default to contracted airport hotels; also no **daytime-sleep** support or odd-hour food. *BLIND-SPOT · conditional*
- **VX** → lost on a **privacy failure** (recognized/exposed, no discreet entry, a fuss made) far more than on an upgrade. A bad discretion experience = never returns, tells peers. *BLIND-SPOT · Y, high*

**Topic 4 · Silent complaints** ★ core gold
- **CW** → can't sleep in the day (light, corridor/HVAC noise, housekeeping knocks); no food at their odd hours; an unreliable wake-up (mission-critical). *BLIND-SPOT · conditional · high-if-present*
- **VX** → the "VIP amenity" they didn't want, a greeting that drew attention, a privacy slip — silent, because a VIP won't complain, they just don't return. *BLIND-SPOT · Y, high*

**Topic 6 · The in-house convenience gap** ★★ headline (additive only)
- **CW** → odd-hour food + a reliable wake-up + uniform pressing — extends the same grab-and-go/sundries gap to crew hours. *ADDITIVE GAP · conditional*
- **VX** → wants off-menu / in-room / discreet food, not the dining room. *ADDITIVE GAP · Y* (still: never judge the restaurant — additive only)

**Topic 9 · Cheap delights — the no-cost lever** ★ core gold
- **CW** → a guaranteed odd-hour wake-up; a genuinely dark, quiet daytime room; a packed meal. *BLIND-SPOT · conditional*
- **VX** → staff briefed to *not* make a fuss; a single named contact; the side door offered without being asked. Discretion is free. *BLIND-SPOT · Y, high*

**Topic 13 · Departure & timing**
- **CW** → departures at any hour; the wake-up + odd-hour transport is the whole game. *BLIND-SPOT · conditional*
- **VX** → flexible, private, on-their-terms; a standard checkout flow can expose them. *MIS-WEIGHT · Y*

## 14 · Crew: the daytime-sleep / reliability blind spot *(new — conditional gold)*

**Prior (strong): "Crew isn't us — that's the airport."** Likely **partly correct.**

The hotel is built around a **day-active business rhythm**; crew need the **inverse** — sleep during the day, eat and depart at odd hours, an absolutely reliable wake-up (a missed wake-up is a missed flight, an airline incident). If the desk sees crew volume, these are unmet by design:
- **Daytime sleep** — blackout, an enforced do-not-disturb, a quiet floor, no daytime housekeeping knock. *BLIND-SPOT*
- **Odd-hour food** — arriving 2am / departing 3am with nothing open. *BLIND-SPOT (= Topic 6 at crew hours)*
- **A guaranteed wake-up** — mission-critical reliability, not a courtesy. *BLIND-SPOT · high*
- **Uniform care** — fast pressing/laundry. *BLIND-SPOT*

> **Integrity flag:** the headline crew finding may simply be **"crew is not a meaningful segment for this property — your instinct holds"** (a CONFIRM). Only if the desk reports real crew volume do the needs above become reportable gold. **Do not manufacture a crew finding from a thin segment.**

## 15 · VIP: recognition vs discretion — the contradiction *(new — the VX headline)*

**Prior (strong): "VIP care = visible recognition"** — the upgrade announced, the manager greeting, the welcome amenity, the PMS VIP flag. The GM's whole VIP program is built on **being seen and celebrated.**

**What the desk sees (the likely contradiction):** for much of this segment — senior execs, anyone travelling incognito, celebrities — the thing they actually value is the **opposite of recognition**: discretion, privacy, invisibility, *not* being made a fuss of. The well-meant "VIP treatment" can be the very thing that annoys them or exposes them.
- **The contradiction:** VIP ≠ celebration; for many, VIP = **discretion**. A recognition-based playbook can backfire on the highest-value guests. *CONTRADICTION · Y, high — IF it clears the floor*
- **The operational blind spot:** a private/side entrance, a single trusted contact, staff briefed *not* to fuss, security/exposure management, off-the-record requests handled without question — none of which a PMS VIP flag captures. *BLIND-SPOT · Y*
- **The recognition blind spot:** the truly important guest is often *not* flagged (a quiet board member, an incognito celebrity); the desk spots them, the system doesn't. *BLIND-SPOT · Y* (ties to instrument P25)

> **Integrity flag:** a sharp luxury GM may already know "VIPs want discretion." For *this* branded-urban-business GM, whose VIP program is a loyalty-tier + amenity model, it is plausibly gold — but grade it **conservatively** and let the pilot confirm. The celebrity slice is **thin**; the gold rides mostly on **execs + incognito-important** guests, where volume is real.

## Where the gold concentrates (updated)
1. **The in-house convenience gap (Topic 6)** — now extended to crew hours (CW) and discreet in-room (VX). Still the headline; still additive.
2. **Silent complaints + defection (4, 5)** — now including the **VX privacy-failure silent churn** (high-value, invisible to the review score).
3. **The Seattle logistical segments (3, 9, 12, 13)** — cruise/event/medical.
4. **Cheap delights (9)** — now including **VX discretion** (free) and **CW reliable wake-ups** (free).
5. **NEW — VIP recognition-vs-discretion (§15)** — a strong CONTRADICTION candidate, conservatively graded.

## Where there is NO / conditional gold (suppress or gate)
- **Crew (CW)** — **conditional.** Likely a non-segment for a downtown house; the honest first finding may be "not your segment." No gold unless volume clears the floor.
- **Celebrity slice of VX** — too thin to clear the floor alone; the VX gold rides on execs + incognito-important guests.
- (Unchanged from v0.3: fitness center, destination fee, comp-set, table stakes.)

## Honest caveats (updated)
- The two new personas are **less certain** than the original six — they were not in the constructed baseline and carry a real **"does this segment exist here in volume?"** question. CW especially may CONFIRM the GM's "not us" prior.
- Per the integrity rule: where the segment is thin, the floor will (correctly) suppress it. **A suppressed crew/VIP finding is the system working, not a gap to fill.**
- The VX discretion-contradiction is the strongest new bet, but a discerning GM may partly hold it already — grade conservatively until the pilot lands.
